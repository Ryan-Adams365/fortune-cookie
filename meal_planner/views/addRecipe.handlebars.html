About Me

Background

I started the Oregon State Computer Science program in Summer 2019. In my first undergraduate degree I studied Psychology with plans to go into the medical field. In pursuit of that goal, I worked as an Emergency Medical Scribe at a local hospital and as a Case Manager at the pharmaceutical company Genentech. However, after a couple years of working, I decided that I was not excited by the idea of becoming a medical professional. Once I came to that conclusion, my new goals were unclear and I just continued aimlessly working at Genentech.

My new passion came when I brought up the idea of automating certain menial parts of our work. I knew it could be done because I had always had an interest in programming and, over the years, I had coded small projects as a hobby. The idea was popular amongst the managers and, after some corporate bureaucracy, I was given the role of Project Technical Lead and allowed to lead an interdepartmental team to implement my idea. The project ended in a huge success and was given an internal award for innovation.

That project lit a fire underneath me and, since that time, my new goal has been to become a software engineer!
